We provide a simple cut elimination proof for the interpretability logic of IL. To achieve this, we introduce a traditional Gentzen-
style sequent calculus for IL and a non-wellfounded version of it. The
non-wellfounded calculus makes it possible to avoid diagonal formulas.
Hence, we can give a simple argument based on a general proof-theoretic
method for calculi of this kind. Our results provide a useful basis for
further research; in particular, they will allow us to establish uniform
interpolation for IL.
